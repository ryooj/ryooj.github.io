SonaMat

The xLAB: Experience Design & Techology Lab explores ways to weave digital information into physical space.

Overview

SonaMat is a fitness mattress that provides personalized fitness guidance. I recruited four designers and engineers to build a system comprised of four components:

  • A physical interface that measures user's movement and delivers guidance through lights
  • A mobile app that shows user's performance
  • A web app that allows content providers like Comcast to create adaptive content
  • A cloud server that analyzes user's movement data and personalizes content delivery

Inspiration

Current content distribution networks provide limited interaction for fitness and wellness activities. The delivery is one-way only and the modules are the same for all users.

The goal of SonaMat is to increase user engagement with fitness activities through fitness content personalization. Below is the SmartMat (similar)'s demo video.

System Implementation

Download our paper published in IEEE to view technical details.

  1. Define your contruct through interviews, surveys, and focus group. What does "personalization" mean in fitness programs?
    • Temporal Personalization adjusts the pace of the content based on performance analytics.
    • Spatial Personalization adjusts the delivery in physical interfaces such as mattress based on user's body metrics including height, weight, and ape index.
    • Procedural Personalization adjusts the difficulty of the content based on performance analytics.

  2. Build a physical interface that measures user's movement and delivers guidance through lights. Collect data, and define product specs and optimal sensor resolution.
  3. Develop fitness pose recognition and evaluation algorithms.
  4. Build an infrastructure.
  5. Build a web app that allows content providers to create adaptive content by tagging content with signals, like subtitles. Build a mobile app that provide personalized feedback to users.
  6. Test, receive feedback, rinse, and repeat.

  7. Bridge the gap between conceptual prototype and manufacturing of the physical interface.
