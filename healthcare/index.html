Human Factors

I introduce human factors engineering principles to design a system or interface.

Cognitive Task Analysis

Understanding the principles of decision-making, cognitive recognition, and cognitive recall is important to design delightful user experience. User experience is not a layer or component of a product or service. It's about the design of whole systems and their interconnections.

My general design principles are:

    1. Understand information flow. Identify and remove cognitive barriers and overloads.
    2. Know user's intuitive perception of your product. Align it with his or her interaction with the technology used.
    3. Make three things clear at the time of an interaction: past, present, and future of your product.
    4. Measure and feel. Measure your feel. Test and improve.
